FRESHMEN SOCIAL CAMP
12 - 15 June 2020 cancelled
Hey there, are you a freshman hoping to settle into university life? Join us at our Freshmen Social Camp for lots of fun and exciting adventures! Forge new friendships and enjoy the many activities that we have planned for you! During this camp, close-knitted friendships are sure to bud between the your orientation group and house mates! Come and join us to experience Sports Day, Beach Day, Mystery Night and more!
Our freshies are already warming up to their OGs and our friendly OGLs are here to make sure everyone feels included in games.
Freshmen will be exposed to IFG sports during sports day to encourage them to represent SOC and play for us. What better way to try our new sports than during sports day!
Join us for a splash at the beach. Enjoy yourself in the sun playing games with your new friends!
At the end of the camp, friendships are forged not only within the orientation groups and house but also beyond! Swee lah!
Here's what our seniors say about FSC!

FSC was my first camp in uni and it was a very good experience for me! The seniors were very welcoming and made all the camp activities very fun and engaging and i managed to make many close friends that have stayed in touch since then. Destinee Ow, Y1 Information Systems

FSC was one of the best camps I experienced in uni! I had so much fun during the camp and got to know many people through the camp. Really had zero regrets signing up for FSC because it was also a stepping stone to joining other activities such as IFG. Loo Huilin, Y1 Business Analytics

I went in with the mindset of having no expectations and to just have fun. Not only was the camp engaging, I had the opportunity to meet a ton of people who helped shape my experience and create a fulfilling journey. This camp helped me to forge bonds with some of my closest friends to date and I encourage everyone to give it a go! Christopher Mervyn, Y1 Computer Science

In retrospect, I do not regret signing up for FSC. Through FSC, I really met so many people across the various courses in SOC. Some of which became my study buddies, some as lobangs for good deals and the smarter ones as grade savers :'). I am also grateful for the OGLS as they were able to sound and timely advice for all the troubles I had assimilating in NUS. Kor Ming Soon, Y1 Computer Science

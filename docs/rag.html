RAG

Looking for a fun yet meaningful way to kickstart your life in NUS? Join SoC Receive-and-Give (RAG)! Forge friendships and make memories this summer as we put together a performance for everyone to enjoy.
Here, we construct large-scale props and floats from scratch.
Get to paint or be painted in preparation for the performance!
Try your hand at designing vibrant and creative costumes!
This is the opportunity to unleash your potential - no prior experience is required!
So what are you waiting for? Sign up now!
Here's what our seniors say about RAG!

COM RAG was the most memorable way to kick off my time here in SoC! It was a great way to meet new people and make new friends ahead of the school term. Aside from making the floats and props from scratch, I got to make lasting memories with my new friends!! (-: The amAzeballs end product also made my experience super rewarding and something I’m proud to say I was a part of. I loved my rag experience and I’m sure your experience will be just as great or even better!! Loh Ying Xin, Year 1 Information Systems

COM RAG was truly an unforgettable and fulfilling experience for me. The countless hours we spent having fun together, blasting music late into the night and having heart to heart talks made for a meaningful orientation experience and the friendships I made during RAG with other freshman and seniors alike are still going strong even today! You won’t regret joining COM RAG! Samuel Fang, Year 1 Computer Science

Participating in MAC gave me the opportunity for new experiences such as making costumes from scratch and doing makeup for the dancers, which I really enjoyed! The long hours spent together allowed me to make new friends while having fun as well. It also gave me a sense of belonging in SoC and made the school term more pleasant when I see familiar faces around! Tan Si Jing, Year 1 Business Analytics

COM RAG is and continues to be the highlight of my university life. RAG was a journey that was not easy, with the multitude of practices per week, while also contributing to the building of floats, but it was worth every second of it. Besides learning to dance and building floats, the friendships forged through the two are irreplaceable. If you are someone looking to start your university life off with a blast, this is it. Jay Chua, Year 1 Computer Science
